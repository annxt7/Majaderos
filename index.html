The three of them are going to fight the grumpy count. Because he wanted to take the balloons away from the children. Idaira And Marina

Idaira, a dreamy 7-year-old girl, excitedly wakes up her older sister Marina, 11, to go buy a giant candy as a birthday gift for her mother. Despite Marina's caution, they get lost looking for the store and run into a mysterious older lady who promises to have the biggest candy in a supposedly special store. The woman guides them to an abandoned house, where Idaira, impulsive and trusting, enters alone and falls into a trap. The lady turns out to be dangerous and kidnaps her. Marina, who had been left outside out of mistrust, bravely rescues her after hearing her screams. Finally, they both escape and walk away safely, understanding that safety and family love are more important than any gift.

Nayara plays as young sister
Marta plays as older sister and old lady

Rapunzel and Clara

Rapunzel prefers creating art instead of studying, so her parents punish her by locking her in a tower without technology. Her friend Clara secretly visits her and helps convince her parents that she can learn in her own way. In the end, Rapunzel finds a balance between studying and expressing herself creatively—all thanks to Clara’s support.

The Influencer Ciga30 and the Ant Lua

This tale of "The Influencer Ciga30 and the Ant Lua" is a story that conveys a lesson about work, effort, responsibility, and foresight. In it, the grasshopper spends all her time singing and enjoying herself, while the ant dedicates herself to gathering food, helping with household chores, and school tasks. When summer arrives and the school year ends, the grasshopper, who has not fulfilled her responsibilities, finds herself very sad and will miss out on summer vacation. The lesson of this story is that excessive use of social media brings negative consequences by losing focus on responsibility and constant effort.

Yaremi plays as Ciga30
Rosario plays as Ant Lua

The Girl who cried broke

This short film is based on the traditional tale “The Boy Who Cried Wolf”. It talks about lies and loss of credibility, where a girl ruins her friendship with her friend because of unnecessary lies about money. She always cries because she has no money, but when she truly needed it, nobody was there. Will she figure out how to earn back her trust? Will she be able to get out of the money problem?

Inés plays as Patri the liar
Nira plays as Mary
Ylenia plays as friend and waitress
